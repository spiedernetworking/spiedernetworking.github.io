About This Website

Throughout the fun of coding freely, I often debate if anyone will ever be able to see my work. My thing is, even if it turns out I only inspire one person to create what they want; from the next big tech company; to the next big shot freelancer, I would be happy so in the event I can I build this. Originally this site started as a Replit project beginning with the name, 'Network Killer'. Honestly, I was hoping to be the next big hacker or something like that. Now as I make my way down this path from 'Bonfire Productions', to 'Spieder Networking', I've realized I don't wish to be that person (or get in major trouble). So I worked night after night to create this simple website for others to see.

What was my own inspiration?

Well, that story falls back to middle school. During that time I had a major addiction to my console. Now I'm not proud to say I was a couch potato, a person who rarely came out to do anything but eat, shower, and go to school. Gaming was what I said was my 'hobby', but I've come to realize I was addicted to the way it felt to control everything with my controller. All actions I made changed the story and let me escape from the real world. Now most might say I did a complete 180 (not 360 or you are still facing the same way). I am more interactive and not always trying to escape life as I often would. During all of this gaming, I wanted the power of a real game that was made for me. So I searched and wouldn't you know it coding was the primary start to that.

With all of this what is my major goal?

Well, I've asked myself the same question. Why do this with the chance no one notices? Well if I made this up on the spot or not the answer is because even if no one else notices my work I still want to see my progress. A visual of where I started to where I can go in the future.

What should be your first steps?

Even though I am not a complete expert I do want to say my peace on this. Start by asking yourself, 'Why and what do I want to do this for?' In all honesty, your answer probably won't match mine, but in the end that's not the point of the question. The point is to find your reason and encouragement to go down this path. Once you have found that out ask yourself again after a while. Your answer may very well change just like my own. It may be because it's fun or maybe it is because you found a hidden talent with it, but either way, ask yourself why every once in a while to keep your encouragement in mind at all times.
